## Key assumptions

* Would-be travellers will not travel if their symptoms have progressed to the point they require hospitalisation
* Screening at exit and entry are independent processes
* Probability of detection by screening does not depend on intensity of symptoms
* Estimated proportions are averages. Substantial variation in proportions are expected if only a small number of travellers are considered
* Travellers will self-report only when they become severely symptomatic during their travel
* Travellers may become severely symptomatic either from a latent state (having boarded the plane with a latent infection) or from a symptomatic state (having gone undetected by the exit screening)
* Travellers developing severe symptoms during their travel will be hospitalised without entry screening
* Asymptomatically infected travellers remain asymptomatic throughout their entire infection
* All symptomatic travellers will eventually be reported

## Bundibugyo ebolavirus (BDBV) specific notes

* Onset-to-hospitalisation parameters are derived from the Bayesian re-analysis of the 2012 Isiro (DRC) outbreak line list (Rosello et al. 2015; Funk & Abbott 2026)
* The incubation period (infection to symptom onset) for BDBV is estimated from the 2007 Bundibugyo (Uganda) outbreak: mean 6.3 days (95% CI: 5.2–7.3; MacNeil et al. 2010). Nash et al. (2024, *Lancet Infect Dis*) systematic review confirms this as the primary BDBV-specific estimate and reports a between-study range of 5.7–11.3 days across three estimates; this between-estimate SD of ~2.8 days is used as the uncertainty prior in the simulation
* Default flight duration is set to 12 hours, reflecting a typical DRC or Uganda to UK connecting itinerary
* The proportion of asymptomatic BDBV infections is set to 5% as a conservative upper estimate; EVD in general is associated with very low asymptomatic rates
